Reflecting on Workforce Preparation: Entering the CTE Film Production class at Burroughs High School, I initially viewed filmmaking as a purely artistic pursuit. However, this course quickly revealed that production is as much about project management and technical troubleshooting as it is about aesthetics. Working on tight deadlines for class assignments helped me develop a professional work ethic that is required in any high-stakes career field.

The collaborative nature of film sets has been a major takeaway. In the workforce, you are rarely a "solo player." Film production forced me to communicate technical needs clearly with my crew members—from adjusting the Kelvin temperature on a light to discussing the pace of a scene. This ability to articulate complex ideas to a team is a skill I will carry into my future career in technology.

Furthermore, the technical rigors of the class have mirrored the challenges I face in coding and 3D modeling. When a camera rig fails or audio has too much gain, you have to troubleshoot in real-time. This "failure-positive" mindset—learning to fix things on the fly—is exactly what prepares a student for the unpredictable nature of the modern workforce.

Beyond technical skills, CTE Film has emphasized the importance of professional "soft skills." Managing equipment worth thousands of dollars and respecting production schedules taught me accountability. These are the traits that employers look for regardless of the industry, and having a year of experience managing these responsibilities has significantly increased my confidence in my professional value.

As I move forward into my goals in computer science and data analysis, I realize that film production was the perfect "sandbox" for learning how to build complex systems. Whether it is a film edit or a codebase, the process of iteration—drafting, receiving feedback, and refining—is identical. This class didn't just teach me how to use a camera; it taught me how to be a professional creator in a digital world.

Ultimately, Burroughs CTE Film Production provided a bridge between classroom learning and real-world application. It allowed me to take ownership of my projects from concept to completion. I am leaving this class not just with a portfolio of films, but with a toolkit of management and production strategies that will serve as the foundation for my future career.
